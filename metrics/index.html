Beyond yield: regulatory implications. The Howey test (SEC's framework for determining if something is a security) asks: "Do profits come from the efforts of others?" Our answer: What others? The code runs. Team can't touch it. Distributions are mathematical, not discretionary.

Technical ‚Ä¢ HOW

Euler Accumulator

Fees are tracked per token via an on-chain accumulator. When you claim, you receive your proportional share of all fees since your last claim. No human decides. No approval needed. Math distributes.

Feature ‚Ä¢ WHAT

ETH Rewards, Not Tokens

Stakers receive ETH, a commodity, not a project token. No inflation schedule. No emission curve. No dilution. Token is your membership card. ETH is your share of activity.

Advantage ‚Ä¢ WHY

Utility, Not Security

Howey's "efforts of others" prong fails when code distributes and team is absent from the reward mechanism. The token grants access and governance. The ETH comes from trading activity, not team promises.

Self-compounding mechanics enable creative token ecosystems that stay regulatory-sound. Reward is ETH. Token has utility as anchor of decisions and values, not as a security-behaving money machine. Verify, don't trust.
