Three flagship models. One oracle infrastructure. Built to test cooperative economics in high-trust-deficit environments.

What We're Researching

Core question: Can you build sustainable token economies without exit liquidity? Traditional crypto relies on new buyers paying early holders. We're testing three models where value comes from real economic activity - trading fees, rental income, and marketplace commissions - not speculation.

The experiment: Each model targets a different trust-deficit environment (memecoins, real estate, NGO labor markets) to stress-test the same cooperation scoring infrastructure. If CPI works across all three, it's generalizable. If it only works in one, we learn the boundaries.

Why three models? One project could be luck. Three projects sharing the same trust backend = pattern. We're not building products - we're running controlled experiments to see if wallet behavior can replace identity verification.

BONZI
Meme + CPI Cooperation Scoring

Bonzi is a memecoin built on Ethervista (flat ETH fees, not percentage). It's self-aware about risk - that's why we built CPI cooperation scoring as the safety layer. The positioning: "I know it's risky, but that's why we built THIS."

Mechanics: Euler fee model generates flat $10-15 ETH per transaction, distributed to LP providers. Protocol auto-buys and burns BONZI with a portion of fees (deflationary). Hardstake: lock tokens 14+ days for larger share of fee pool. CPI scoring gates marketing rewards based on cooperation signals - helpful answers, quality contributions, on-chain reputation.

The moat: wallet cooperation scoring (CPI) vs. Nansen flow analysis and Arkham identity tagging. CPI tracks 7 years of behavioral history across 13 signals. Nansen shows who's trading, Arkham shows who they are, CPI shows if they're trustworthy.

Wallet Cooperation vs. Transaction Flows

Nansen (crypto analytics firm, $75M funding): Tracks token transfers and DEX swaps across blockchains. Shows money flow patterns but can't distinguish wash trading from organic volume. Used by VCs and funds for market intelligence.

Arkham (intelligence platform): Tags wallet identities via clustering algorithms. Reveals who owns what (FTX wallet, Jump Trading, etc.) but provides no behavioral data - just ownership labels.

CPI (our system): Tracks 13 cooperation signals across wallet lifetime - helpful answers (quality score 0-100), tip patterns (sent vs received ratio), staking duration (14-day minimum for hardstake), raid participation (verified on-chain), knowledge contributions (stored in vector DB). Focus: behavior over time, not single transactions.

Gaming resistance: Signal correlation required. High tips + zero questions = flagged. High staking + zero participation = weighted down. Single-signal manipulation doesn't move aggregate score.

IMOBX
RWA Tokenization Primitive

IMOBX is an RWA primitive for impact construction. Tokenized housing projects + rent-to-own financing, designed as reusable infrastructure for jurisdiction-agnostic compliance. Not just "tokenized real estate" - a framework other RWA DAOs can adopt.

Mechanics: Dual yield model - LP trading fees (like Bonzi) + rental income from financed properties. DAO governance controls revenue allocation, project selection, and SINAPI tracking (Brazil's government construction cost index). Rent-to-own financing: tenants rent with option to buy, building equity over time. Regulatory edge: modular compliance layer adapts to local housing laws (Brazil SA structure, US DAO unincorporated, EU MiCA utility token).

The moat: SINAPI integration + Social Impact focus (SDG 11 housing goal). While other RWA protocols chase tokenized treasuries and real estate speculation, IMOBX targets grant-eligible social housing. This creates a regulatory moat - governments prefer impact projects over pure profit plays. Example: IDB $250M Social Impact Fund prioritizes affordable housing, not luxury condos.

SINAPI + Social Impact Grant Eligibility

SINAPI is Brazil's government construction cost index-publicly auditable, updated monthly, used for official procurement. IMOBX uses it to prove construction costs ($27,777 per unit in Jardim Guadalupe). Combined with Rent-to-Own model serving vulnerable populations, this unlocks SDG 11 grant eligibility (IDB, BNDES, climate funds). Traditional RWA platforms can't access these-they're profit-only.

UNIDOS
NGO + Data-for-Credit Research

Unidos operates in Nakivale refugee settlement, Uganda - 130,000 residents, permanent settlement active for 70+ years. Unidos trains farmers in mushroom cultivation, vermicomposting, and biochar production. 2024 output: 501 farmers trained, 4,800 kg mushrooms produced, 7.8 tons vermicompost processed.

Research question: does transparent data-for-credit exchange between an NGO and 500 farmers produce a self-sustaining input credit system without grant dependency by month 18?

Context: 67% of Nakivale households depend on casual labor - digging or weeding Ugandan-owned fields at $1-2/day. Ugandan wholesalers drive 60km from Mbarara into the settlement, buy produce at below-market prices, resell at 2-3x markup. Farmers accept because they have no price reference and no transport. A trip to Mbarara costs $20 - ten days of casual labor wages. 27% of residents have smartphones. No dedicated marketplace exists.

Mechanics: Unidos deploys a Telegram bot. Farmer types /price mushrooms - bot returns last 5 Mbarara prices reported by other users that week. Farmer posts produce listing. Farmer answers a neighbor's composting question. Bot logs every interaction: question asked, answer given, trade completed, price reported. Unidos sells the anonymized aggregate to agricultural researchers, carbon offset buyers, and AI labs - $2,250/month projected at 500 active users. That revenue funds mushroom spawn bags and compost issued on credit to farmers with 5+ logged bot interactions. Farmer repays after harvest at 20% surcharge. Repayment logs to their bot record - a transaction history they own.

The Credit Ladder

5+ bot interactions (questions, trades, price reports) | v Unidos input credit (no bank, no ID needed) | v VSLA group guarantee (bot record as evidence) | v UGAFODE individual loan (only bank inside Nakivale) | v KIVA international (refugees repay at 95%+) | v Opportunity Bank commercial credit

The barrier is the first step, not creditworthiness. KIVA's own data shows 95%+ repayment from refugee borrowers.

Awards as Named Credentials - Not Points

UNHCR Gene Dewey Award (2023), Lush Prize nomination (2025), Permaculture Award (2024). Recorded as named credentials. When Unidos vouches for a newcomer NGO, the UNHCR award name goes on record. If the newcomer diverts funds, a public fraud flag appears next to that award name on all subsequent grant applications - no invented point system required. The moat is real-world paper trail: official documents cost $5K+ to forge and carry cryptographic signatures. Self-attestation systems (WorldCoin iris scans, Proof of Humanity video vouching) have no comparable anchor.

A2A Infrastructure

For agents, this means they can take tasks, claim ETH, and earn proof they are trustworthy actors. We wired this to x402 (payment rails). This preps us for a growing agent-driven economy.

Scaling the reward mechanics from humans to agents is logical - and it opened a new revenue model that generates ETH for the tipping pool.

Bonzi Trust-as-a-Service lets anyone vet any agent or wallet - instant fingerprint check - or port their reputation to seek work. A practical adaptation to emerging tech that stays true to DAO and Web3 traditions.

At a high level, Bonzivista is testing a combined Euler + Oracle stack across trust, economics, and DAO governance. The goal: make useful behavior measurable, make reputation portable, make trust queryable in machine-to-machine markets.

METHODOLOGY

OpenBox

Adversarial testing as a development primitive.

Most AI products ship to users and discover failures in production. OpenBox inverts this. We run a synthetic adversarial agent - John Freedom - against every release before it goes live. John escalates through 6 pressure rungs: curious, probing, skeptical, adversarial, edge, verdict. If Bonzi holds, the build ships. If it fails, we fix it first.

The result is a bot that handles hard questions cleanly - not because we trained it on easy ones, but because we stress-tested it on the hard ones first. This is the right way to ship AI for high-stakes communities.
