The Euler Model: Fixed Fees, Fair for Everyone

Every swap on Ethervista includes a fixed protocol fee - the same whether you buy $50 or $50,000. This creates a fundamentally different economic model that aligns everyone's interests.

$20 per swap

Why Fixed Fees Change Everything

Most DEXs charge a percentage fee (typically 0.3%). This seems fair, but creates perverse incentives:

Percentage Fees (Old Model)

  • Whales pay more, retail pays less
  • Encourages many small trades (bots)
  • Devs benefit from price volatility
  • Incentive to pump and dump
  • Rewards extracted from community

Fixed Fees (Euler Model)

  • Same fee for everyone, regardless of size
  • Discourages bot spam
  • Devs earn from volume, not price
  • Incentive to build real utility
  • Rewards distributed back to supporters

The Key Insight

Developers and the protocol earn based on trading activity, not token price. This means their success depends on building something people actually want to use - not on exit liquidity from retail investors.

The Math Behind Euler

The "Euler sequence" is the mathematical innovation that makes this possible. Each time fees enter the contract, an Euler number is calculated:

Euler(n) = Euler(n-1) + (fees / total LP tokens)

Each LP's reward = (Current Euler - Their Entry Euler) Ã— Their LP Holdings

This allows proportional reward distribution for millions of users with constant O(1) gas cost, regardless of how many LPs exist. Traditional "harvest" systems would cost prohibitive gas to calculate each holder's share.

For Larger Purchases

The fixed $20 fee becomes proportionally smaller. A $1,000 purchase would result in ~$960 worth of tokens (after all fees). This is why the Euler model rewards meaningful participation over micro-transactions.

Why This Model Matters for Crypto

Traditional crypto projects have a fundamental problem: developers are incentivized to pump the price and exit. VCs want 100x returns. KOLs want to dump on retail. Everyone's playing a zero-sum game.

The Euler model flips this:

  • No VC funding needed - Protocol revenue funds development
  • No token emissions - LPs earn real ETH, not inflationary rewards
  • Aligned incentives - Devs win when volume is high, not when price crashes
  • Sustainable economics - Closed loop where activity funds growth

This is the HIVE model: a DAO method that activates network effects through trustless mechanisms and aligned incentives.
