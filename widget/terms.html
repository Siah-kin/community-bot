The $20 Fee. And Why It Exists.

Every swap on Ethervista costs $20. Not a percentage. A flat fee, paid in ETH. This page explains where that money goes and why this model works differently than what you're used to.

Bonzi Widget v1.0 · January 2026 · Ethervista Docs

The Short Version

You pay $20 per swap. That $20 gets split between liquidity providers (who earn ETH, not tokens), the protocol treasury, and a buyback mechanism. Developers earn from trading activity. Not from price pumps. Not from dumping on you.

This is different from Uniswap's 0.3% model. On Uniswap, a $50 trade costs $0.15. A $50,000 trade costs $150. Sounds fair until you realize it incentivizes thousands of small bot trades and penalizes real participation.

Heads up: If you're buying less than $200 worth, the fixed fee hits hard. A $50 purchase loses 40% to fees before you even get tokens. This model rewards larger, committed purchases. That's intentional.

Where the $20 Goes

The fee splits between four destinations. You can verify this on-chain.

Liquidity Providers receive the majority. They earn ETH directly. Not governance tokens that inflate. Not rewards that require gas to claim. Actual ETH, calculated per-swap using Euler math (O(1) distribution, no matter how many LPs exist).

Protocol Treasury funds development. No VCs. No token sales. The protocol pays for itself through usage.

Buyback + Burn removes tokens from circulation. Every swap creates deflationary pressure.

$1 to VISTA funds the DEX itself. Flash loans, futures, new features.

Why Fixed Fees

Most DEX fees are percentages. Sounds neutral. But it creates problems:

  • Bots spam thousands of micro-trades. Each one profitable, each one clogging the pool.
  • Developers earn more when prices are volatile. Pump and dump becomes rational.
  • Large purchases get punished. The people who believe most pay the most.

Fixed fees flip the incentives. Bots can't profit on small trades. Developers earn from volume, which means building things people actually use. And your $10,000 purchase pays the same $20 as someone's $100.

The project succeeds when trading activity is high. Not when prices spike. Not when retail gets dumped on. When people actually use the protocol.

That's alignment you can verify on-chain. Not a promise in a whitepaper.

The Euler Math

How do you split fees fairly among thousands of LPs without spending $50 in gas per distribution? Ethervista uses Euler sequences.

Each swap updates a single number: Euler(n) = Euler(n-1) + (fee / totalLP)

Each LP stores when they entered. Their reward = (currentEuler - entryEuler) × holdings

One storage slot. O(1) computation. Works for 10 LPs or 10 million.

This is why LPs get paid in ETH instantly, not in tokens you have to harvest.

What This Isn't For

Small speculative trades. If you want to throw $20 at a memecoin and see what happens, you'll lose most of it to fees. That's by design.

Quick flips. The fee structure discourages in-and-out trading. It rewards holding.

People who don't read terms pages. You're here, so probably not you.
