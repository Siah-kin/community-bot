Educational purpose

A roadmap with development depth

A roadmap should not only state milestones. It should explain the intermediate evidence: what is built, what changed, what failed, and what still needs proof.

Development depth

Show the operating layer

The roadmap explains the actual proof type behind each phase: rows, records, receipts, comparisons, and traceability.

  • Built work is separated from planned work.
  • Evidence is explained in plain language.
  • Correction history is treated as signal, not embarrassment.
Strategy depth

Explain why each phase exists

Each phase should answer what it unlocks, why it matters, what timing depends on, and what it still does not claim.

  • Phase 1 proves the base evidence layer.
  • Phase 2 strengthens decision memory.
  • Later phases support comparison, traceability, and partner posture.
Reviewer orientation

Give reviewers a method

The page should help reviewers follow progress without needing raw engineering access, private review instructions, or unsupported confidence.

  • Current proof status is visible.
  • Next review focus is explicit.
  • Stats stay bounded and easy to compare over time.
Communication standard

Make progress legible

The standard is not announcement frequency. The standard is whether a community can explain the depth of its development and strategy without overstating what is ready.

  • Less vague momentum language.
  • More proof-aware communication.
  • Clearer boundaries before scale.

Phase 1 detail

What the first proof pack actually proves

Phase 1 is the deepest section because it is the proven section. Its value is specific: the system produced records, not just plans.

Proof 1

Experiment evidence

The OpenBox/RACI economy path produced an evidence row. Founder meaning: the experiment surface can leave an operating trace.

Proof 2

Shadow trust scoring

The trust side produced shadow scoring evidence. Founder meaning: scoring can be observed before it becomes stronger authority.

Proof 3

Contributor credit

A confirmed bug signal produced credit evidence. Founder meaning: useful user feedback can become a visible contribution record.

Proof 4

Escalation confirmation

A serious support escalation produced a confirmation event. Founder meaning: critical support moments can leave durable records.

Why the correction matters

One Phase 1 closure was challenged because the escalation proof needed stronger confirmation. The pack was corrected, checked again, and re-closed only after the required event record was verified. That is a stronger signal than a clean story with no error memory.

Boundaries

What this roadmap does not claim

Early proof is valuable because it is narrow. The page should help investors and contributors understand progress without turning early evidence into a bigger claim.

  • Phase 1 does not mean public launch readiness.
  • Phase 1 does not mean final reward economics are complete.
  • Phase 1 does not mean final trust scoring is ready for user outcomes.
  • Phase 1 does not mean all support paths are automated.
  • Phase 2-5 work remains pending until the proof gates are satisfied.
  • Contributor onboarding is not a shortcut to formal authority.
  • Internal review mechanics and sensitive operating details stay off this public roadmap surface.
  • Roadmap language follows proof; it does not create proof by itself.
Review questions

Questions for ongoing review

A living roadmap should provide repeatable questions, not only a timeline. These questions help reviewers understand whether progress is deepening or merely being restated.

Current question: memory quality

The next useful signal is whether past decisions become easier to review and cite.

  • Can decisions be traced back to bounded sources?
  • Can reviewers understand why a decision happened?
  • Can later work avoid repeating old mistakes?

Next question: external comparison

After memory improves, the stronger question is how BonziVista compares to nearby trust, agent, and community systems.

  • What is genuinely different?
  • Which claims are still too early?
  • Where does the proof base need more depth?

Constant question: claim discipline

The roadmap is useful only if it keeps separating evidence from ambition.

  • Proof should lead the story.
  • Boundaries should stay visible.
  • Public language should change when proof changes.
