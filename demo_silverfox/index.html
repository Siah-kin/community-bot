The loop

One path, four steps. Each step leaves a record.

ticket → case → reviewed reply → receipt

1
Ticket. You send what broke. A bug, a broken button, a confusing flow.
2
Case. Your ticket becomes a case. Similar reports may be grouped.
3
Reviewed reply. A draft answer may be prepared. A person reviews it before it reaches you.
4
Receipt. A receipt records the path: the ticket, the review, the reply, the outcome.

Send a useful ticket

  1. Open the bot.
  2. Type /ticket.
  3. Say what you did, what you expected, what happened.
  4. Add a screenshot or a link if it helps.
Never send seed phrases, private keys, passwords, or payment details. Keep those to yourself. A bug report never needs them.

After you send it

  • It becomes a case.
  • Similar reports may be grouped.
  • A draft reply may be prepared.
  • A person reviews it.
  • You get a reply when the answer is ready.
  • Target: within 48h.

Receipts

A receipt records the path. The ticket, the review, the reply, the outcome. It is something you can point to later, not a promise you have to take on faith.

FAQ

Is this a chatbot?

It answers, but a reply that affects you is reviewed by a person first. It is not a fire-and-forget bot.

Does it auto-send replies?

No. A person reviews the reply before it goes out. Live confirmation of that send is still being checked.

What should I put in a bug report?

What you did, what you expected, what happened. A screenshot or a link helps a lot.

How long does a reply take?

The target is within 48h.

Can I earn from reporting bugs?

Not yet. That comes later. There is no automatic reward today.

What should I never send?

Seed phrases, private keys, passwords, payment details. Keep those to yourself.
