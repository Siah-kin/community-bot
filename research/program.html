Research Program

Turning extraction loops into repair loops

Bonzi tests a simple idea: reward systems that usually drain people can be redesigned to fund real work, useful trust, and healthier choices.

Vision

The long-term goal is a community economy where incentives point toward repair instead of extraction. Revenue should come from real activity: trades, housing, marketplace work, verified help, and accountable services.

Bonzi is not trying to hide risk behind branding. The program names what exists, what is still a design, and what evidence would prove us wrong.

The test

Can a token system reward useful behavior without becoming a loop that only pays old participants with new participants' losses?

Mission

Run small, falsifiable experiments. Publish the stage. Keep the data contestable. Stop when a safety boundary breaks.

The research program turns each product into a stress test: trust scoring in Bonzi, real-asset accountability in IMOBX, data-for-credit in Unidos, and habit-support rewards in Touch Grass & Earn.

The meta-thesis

Many online systems use rewards to keep people clicking, betting, or chasing losses. The same structure can be pointed somewhere else: toward proof of help, proof of work, proof of repayment, or proof of a better habit.

Touch Grass & Earn tests this at the habit layer. It is inspired by contingency management: verify a target behavior, credit points, and let the streak multiplier rise. It is not medical treatment, not healthcare advice, and not a claim to treat addiction.

What would count as progress?

Progress is not hype or price movement. Progress means more verified useful actions, fewer gaming patterns, clear user consent, and a reward budget that does not depend on participant losses.

Bonzi/SIAH application lanes

Bonzi/SIAH is a backend protocol for trust and governance. Each lane below is a staged application of that protocol, not a separate product line.

Bonzi: trust from behavior Live

Tests whether wallet behavior, support history, and contribution records can identify cooperation better than hype or wallet size.

IMOBX: real assets Pilot

Tests whether the same trust and governance layer can support rent-to-own housing, audited costs, and local compliance.

Unidos: data for credit Designed

Tests whether farmers with thin records can use verified marketplace activity to open input credit and qualify for better pricing.

The Nakivale farmer training program is real and running. The Bonzi/SIAH bot marketplace layer on top of it is a design, not deployed.

B2B: business coordination Pilot

Tests whether the trust and governance layer can carry coordination, referrals, client and supplier tracking, and trust-backed business workflows. Staged as a Phase 3 pilot, not current go-to-market.

Touch Grass & Earn: habit rewards Phase 3 research/pilot

Tests whether points-first rewards for verified healthy-food purchases can support a better habit without promising treatment or automatic ETH.

Blocked until Raid & Earn is working. This lane does not open before that dependency clears.

OpenBox is the research governor

OpenBox is how we test ourselves before asking anyone else to trust us. It runs adversarial reviews, records failure modes, and keeps releases behind evidence gates.

For Touch Grass & Earn, that means no cash-out bridge without a separate review. For Bonzi, it means trust scores must be contestable. For IMOBX and Unidos, it means claims about housing or credit stay tied to documents, costs, and field evidence.

  • Accountable owner: a named person is responsible for each experiment.
  • Evidence path: every important decision leaves a record.
  • Contest path: users and reviewers can challenge decisions.
  • Bounded stop: the system halts when a safety boundary breaks.

Research rules

Real activity first

Revenue must trace to useful activity, not a promise that later buyers will save earlier users.

Stage labels stay visible

Live, pilot, designed, and future work are different. The page should make that difference obvious.

No medical claims

Habit-support experiments can be useful without claiming to diagnose, treat, or prevent addiction.

Stop rules beat momentum

If incentives cause harm, privacy fails, or funding is not real, the right answer is to halt.

How to read the program

Read the research hub as a map of experiments, not a list of promises. Each page should show what is live, what is being tested, and what would falsify the claim.
