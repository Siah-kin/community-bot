What

Bonzi is a proof-based governance ledger.

Built for communities, brands, token projects, and DAOs, it helps groups make decisions they can prove: who contributed, who verified, who was affected, and what happened next.

Community decisions become stronger when they leave a record that can be reviewed later.

The problem

Online trust breaks when decisions stay informal.

Communities often know that work happened, but they cannot easily show who did it, who checked it, and why a decision followed.

Informal decisions fade

Chat history, private messages, and memory are weak foundations for contribution, dispute, or reward decisions.

AI can amplify authority risk

If one agent can plan, act, and certify itself, the community has speed without a reliable check on power.

Voting is not enough

DAOs and community-led projects need evidence before and after votes: context, verification, consequences, and correction.

What Bonzi is

A shared record for contribution, verification, gratitude, and outcomes.

Bonzi is built for groups that need more than announcements. It gives community work a place to become reviewable.

Bonzi helps communities govern with proof. It records contribution, verification, gratitude, and outcomes so groups can coordinate fairly without trusting one person, one model, or one unchecked system.

The aim is not to replace people. The aim is to make important community work easier to trace, review, and improve.

How it works

Important claims should leave evidence behind.

The method stays simple. Work can begin in conversation, but significant decisions should end in records.

People and agents propose work

A report, question, contribution, dispute, or task enters the system.

Contributions are recorded

The useful signal becomes easier to find later than a message in a crowded chat.

Claims require proof

Important statements should connect to records, reviewers, or outcomes.

Checks challenge self-certification

No actor should be the only source for its own approval.

Outcomes become history

The final decision becomes part of the community record, not a lost explanation.

The three ledgers

Governance, contribution, and gratitude belong together.

Community trust improves when work, review, and recognition can be read as one connected record.

Governance ledger

Decisions, approvals, disputes, verification, escalation, and review history.

Contribution ledger

Work, help, participation, bug reports, useful context, and value created.

Gratitude ledger

Thanks, rewards, recognition, and social proof that a community can remember.

Governance model

Ostrom-compliant governance, in plain terms.

A community commons needs rules people can understand, records people can inspect, and escalation when something becomes sensitive.

Clear roles

People should know who proposed, reviewed, approved, objected, or escalated.

Monitoring and proof

Important work should be visible through records, not private certainty.

Escalation and oversight

Sensitive cases need human review and a path for disagreement.

No unchecked authority

No person, model, or system should be allowed to certify itself without challenge.

Who it is for

Built for groups where community work has real value.

Bonzi is relevant wherever contribution, trust, recognition, and decisions need to become easier to review.

  • DAOs
  • Token communities
  • Creator communities
  • Brands with active communities
  • Open-source or volunteer networks
  • Local or digital commons
Why it is different

Not just chat, voting, or analytics.

Bonzi is not useful because it speaks more. It is useful when it helps a community keep accountable records.

Not just a chatbot

A chatbot can answer. Bonzi should connect important answers to records and review.

Not just a voting tool

A vote records preference. Bonzi should preserve the evidence around why a decision was made.

Not just analytics

Analytics summarize behavior. Bonzi should help a community act, verify, and remember.

Proof, not claims

Bonzi's rule is simple: important claims need evidence.

That is the standard for contributions, rewards, disputes, approvals, corrections, and public progress. A claim becomes stronger when the record behind it becomes stronger.
