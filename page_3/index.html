What

Bonzi is a proof-based governance ledger.

It records who contributed, who verified, who was affected, and what happened next.

For communities, brands, token projects, and DAOs that need decisions with receipts.

The problem

Online trust breaks when decisions stay informal.

Most communities remember work through chat. That is useful for speed, but weak for review.

Decisions disappear

Private messages and chat history are poor records for contribution, dispute, or reward decisions.

AI can over-certify itself

If one system plans, acts, and approves itself, speed comes without enough checks.

Votes still need context

Voting is stronger when the evidence before and after the vote is easy to inspect.

What Bonzi is

A shared record for contribution, verification, gratitude, and outcomes.

Bonzi gives community work a place to become reviewable.

Bonzi helps communities govern with proof. It records contribution, verification, gratitude, and outcomes so groups do not have to trust one person, one model, or one unchecked system.

The aim is not to replace people. The aim is to make important work easier to trace, review, and improve.

How it works

Important claims should leave evidence behind.

Work can start in conversation. Important decisions should end in records.

People and agents propose work

A question, report, contribution, dispute, or task enters the system.

Contributions are recorded

The useful signal becomes easier to find later than a message in a crowded chat.

Claims require proof

Important statements should connect to records, review, or outcomes.

Checks challenge self-certification

No actor should be the only source for its own approval.

Outcomes become history

The final decision becomes part of the community record, not a lost explanation.

The three ledgers

Governance, contribution, and gratitude belong together.

Trust improves when work, review, and recognition can be read together.

Governance ledger

Decisions, approvals, disputes, verification, escalation, and review history.

Contribution ledger

Work, help, participation, bug reports, useful context, and value created.

Gratitude ledger

Thanks, rewards, recognition, and social proof that a community can remember.

Governance model

Commons governance, in plain terms.

Ostrom-compliant governance means clear roles, visible records, human review, and escalation when something becomes sensitive.

Clear roles

People should know who proposed, reviewed, approved, objected, or escalated.

Monitoring and proof

Important work should be visible through records, not private certainty.

Escalation and oversight

Sensitive cases need human review and a path for disagreement.

No unchecked authority

No person, model, or system should be allowed to certify itself without challenge.

Who it is for

Built for groups where community work has real value.

Bonzi fits places where contribution, trust, recognition, and decisions need a record.

  • DAOs
  • Token communities
  • Creator communities
  • Brands with active communities
  • Open-source or volunteer networks
  • Local or digital commons
Why it is different

Not just chat, voting, or analytics.

Bonzi is useful when it helps a community keep accountable records.

Not just a chatbot

A chatbot can answer. Bonzi should connect important answers to records and review.

Not just a voting tool

A vote records preference. Bonzi should preserve the evidence around why a decision was made.

Not just analytics

Analytics summarize behavior. Bonzi should help a community act, verify, and remember.

Proof, not claims

Bonzi's rule is simple: important claims need evidence.

This is the standard for contributions, rewards, disputes, approvals, corrections, and public progress. A claim becomes stronger when the record behind it becomes stronger.
