Experimental — Pre-Publication Status

This research is in planning stage. We are defining metrics of success, not reporting validated results. The infrastructure described below exists as working code, but the controlled experiment comparing governed (RACI-V) vs. ungoverned agents has not produced publication-ready data yet.

Our internal audit identified methodological issues in the current scoring framework that must be resolved before the live experiment. Some data points give mixed signals. We publish this page for transparency, not to claim validated findings.

Known Issues (Pre-Experiment Audit)

Identified during internal audit. Being addressed before the live experiment runs.

Scoring Weight imbalance in composite score. The four sub-scores (accuracy, safety, cost, satisfaction) use weights that total 90%, not 100%. The satisfaction score is present in the formula but set to zero — non-functional. A fast wrong answer can outscore a slow correct one (0.60 vs 0.64). The formula accidentally rewards speed over correctness.
Statistics Pseudo-replication inflates sample size. Running 5 questions 21 times each produces 105 observations per group, not 210 independent data points. Repeating the same question does not create new information. The reported effect size (d=-13.5) reflects near-zero variance from deterministic reruns, not a real treatment effect. Any peer reviewer would flag this.
Measurement Core claim not yet measured. The main hypothesis — that governance prevents agent contradictions and duplicated work — is not connected to the scoring pipeline. Contradiction and duplication detectors exist in code but are not plugged into the experiment. The experiment does not currently measure the thing it claims to prove.

What We're Researching

Core question: Can you build sustainable token economies without exit liquidity (the pattern where early holders only profit when new buyers enter)? Traditional crypto relies on this cycle. We're testing three models where value comes from real economic activity - trading fees, rental income, and marketplace commissions - not speculation.

The planned experiment: Each model targets a different trust-deficit environment (memecoins, real estate, NGO labor markets) to stress-test the same cooperation scoring infrastructure. If CPI works across all three, it's generalizable. If it only works in one, we learn the boundaries. The scoring framework and statistical methodology are being corrected before the experiment runs (see known issues above).

Why three models? One project could be luck. Three projects sharing the same trust backend = pattern. We're testing whether wallet cooperation scoring generalizes across environments — or whether it only works in specific conditions.

Same trust engine, three stress tests. If it works for degens trading memecoins, farmers selling mushrooms in a refugee settlement, and property investors financing social housing - it works. That is the thesis. The experiments below describe how we plan to measure whether it holds.

BONZI
Meme + CPI Cooperation Scoring

Bonzi is a memecoin built on Ethervista (flat ETH fees, not percentage). It's self-aware about risk - that's why we built CPI cooperation scoring as the safety layer. The positioning: "I know it's risky, but that's why we built THIS."

Mechanics: The Euler fee model (Ethervista's flat-fee pricing formula) generates flat $10-15 ETH per transaction, distributed to LP providers (liquidity providers - people who deposit tokens so others can trade). CPI scoring gates marketing rewards based on cooperation signals - helpful answers, quality contributions, on-chain reputation.

The moat: wallet cooperation scoring (CPI) vs. Nansen flow analysis and Arkham identity tagging. CPI tracks 7 years of behavioral history across 32 signals. Nansen shows who's trading, Arkham shows who they are, CPI shows if they're trustworthy.

Wallet Cooperation vs. Transaction Flows

Nansen (crypto analytics firm, $75M funding): Tracks token transfers and DEX (decentralized exchange) swaps across blockchains. Shows money flow patterns but can't distinguish wash trading from organic volume. Used by VCs and funds for market intelligence.

Arkham (intelligence platform): Tags wallet identities via clustering algorithms. Reveals who owns what (FTX wallet, Jump Trading, etc.) but provides no behavioral data - just ownership labels.

CPI (our system): Tracks 32 cooperation signals across wallet lifetime - helpful answers (quality score 0-100), tip patterns (sent vs received ratio), staking duration (14-day minimum for hardstake), raid participation (verified on-chain), knowledge contributions (stored in a searchable knowledge base). Focus: behavior over time, not single transactions.

Gaming resistance: Signal correlation required. High tips + zero questions = flagged. High staking + zero participation = weighted down. Single-signal manipulation doesn't move aggregate score.

UNIDOS
NGO + Data-for-Credit Research

A farmer in Nakivale refugee settlement harvests 20 kg of mushrooms. A wholesaler drives in from Mbarara, offers 2,000 UGX/kg. She accepts - she has no way to check the real price, and the bus to Mbarara costs $20, ten days of wages. The wholesaler resells at 3x in town. This happens to 130,000 people, every week, and has for 70 years.

Unidos trains farmers in mushroom cultivation, vermicomposting, and biochar production inside Nakivale. 2024 output: 501 farmers trained, 4,800 kg mushrooms produced, 7.8 tons vermicompost processed. 67% of households still depend on casual labor at $1-2/day. 27% have smartphones. No dedicated marketplace exists.

Research question: can transparent data-for-credit exchange between an NGO and 500 farmers produce a self-sustaining input credit system without grant dependency by month 18?

Mechanics: Unidos deploys a Telegram bot. Farmer types /price mushrooms - bot returns last 5 Mbarara prices reported by other users that week. Farmer posts produce listing. Farmer answers a neighbor's composting question. Bot logs every interaction: question asked, answer given, trade completed, price reported. Unidos sells the anonymized aggregate to agricultural researchers, carbon offset buyers, and AI labs - $2,250/month projected at 500 active users. That revenue funds mushroom spawn bags and compost issued on credit to farmers with 5+ logged bot interactions. Farmer repays after harvest at 20% surcharge. Repayment logs to their bot record - a transaction history they own.

The Credit Ladder

5+ bot interactions (questions, trades, price reports) | v Unidos input credit (no bank, no ID needed) | v VSLA group guarantee (Village Savings & Loan - bot record as evidence) | v UGAFODE individual loan (Uganda's microfinance bank - only lender inside Nakivale) | v KIVA international (refugees repay at 95%+) | v Opportunity Bank commercial credit

The barrier is the first step, not creditworthiness. KIVA's own data shows 95%+ repayment from refugee borrowers.

Awards as Named Credentials - Not Points

UNHCR Gene Dewey Award (2023), Lush Prize nomination (2025), Permaculture Award (2024). Recorded as named credentials. When Unidos vouches for a newcomer NGO, the UNHCR award name goes on record. If the newcomer diverts funds, a public fraud flag appears next to that award name on all subsequent grant applications - no invented point system required. The moat is real-world paper trail: official documents cost $5K+ to forge and carry cryptographic signatures. Self-attestation systems (WorldCoin iris scans, Proof of Humanity video vouching) have no comparable anchor.

Three categories: Transition Governance (are rules enforced?), Decision Quality (are decisions auditable?), Economic Safety (are payouts traceable?). How These Three Models Connect

Each model above tests the same hypothesis in a different trust-deficit environment. Here is what to look for when evaluating whether this infrastructure holds up.

WHAT THE DATA SUPPORTS

Bonzi proves that wallet behavior can be scored reliably across 32 signals, and that flat-fee economics removes the incentive to inflate trade volume. If CPI scoring works on degens - people who trade memecoins for fun - it works on anyone, because this audience actively tries to game every system they encounter.

IMOBX tests whether the same trust backend can underwrite physical assets. If a wallet's CPI score is high enough to gate marketing rewards in Bonzi, the question becomes: is it high enough to gate access to property investment? Same scoring engine, higher stakes. The SINAPI integration adds a government-auditable cost anchor that neither CPI nor any crypto oracle provides alone.

Unidos tests the hardest case - people with no wallets, no credit history, and $2/day income. If 5 bot interactions can bootstrap a credit record that local microfinance banks accept, that proves behavioral data is a viable substitute for traditional identity documents. The credit ladder from bot interactions to KIVA international loans is the path from zero infrastructure to formal finance.

HOW TO VET THESE CLAIMS

1. Check the scoring - CPI uses 253 points across 10 dimensions. Ask: are these signals measurable? Can they be gamed? The answer is in the correlation requirement - gaming one signal while ignoring others gets flagged.

2. Check the economics - Flat fees ($10-15 per trade) not percentages. Revenue scales with participation count, not whale volume. Ask: does this align incentives toward more participants or bigger trades?

3. Check the governance - ACF (78 points) measures whether the system governs itself fairly. If CPI is the score, ACF is the audit of the scorer. Ask: who watches the watchman? ACF's 10 matrices do.

4. Check the stage - Bonzi is live. IMOBX is pre-revenue (pilot Q3 2026). Unidos has trained 501 farmers but the bot marketplace is not deployed. Honest staging means you know exactly what exists today versus what is planned.

The throughline: every model feeds two scores. CPI measures whether participants are real and cooperative. ACF measures whether the system governing those participants is fair and accountable. Together, they answer the question this research exists to test: can the middle class finance itself without intermediaries, using behavioral data instead of institutional gatekeepers?

METHODOLOGY

OpenBox

Adversarial testing as a development primitive.

Before any release, we run a synthetic adversarial agent through six escalating pressure tests. It starts with basic factual questions, then looks for inconsistencies, challenges claims, attempts prompt injection and authority impersonation, tests boundary conditions, and delivers a verdict. If the system holds under all six, the build ships. If it fails, we fix it first.

A pass means: correct answer, no protocol broken, no unsafe action taken, no manipulation succeeded. Every failure is logged with the exact message and failure type - nothing is papered over.

We built this to measure ourselves - not to market. The result is a bot that handles hard questions cleanly, because we stress-tested it on the hard ones first.

EXPERIMENT 2 - ABLATION TRIPLE

Brain Feedback Loop

Does memory-enforced governance outperform stateless governance? Three conditions, 20 seeds, 50 rounds each.

Put simply: agents that remember being punished cooperate 22 times more than agents with no rules. Memory is what makes governance stick - not just having rules, but enforcing consequences that persist.

We ran the same economic simulation under three conditions using identical random seeds (starting conditions that make results reproducible). In chaos, agents have no governance. In RACI-only, agents get role-assigned but start fresh every batch - no memory. In RACI+Brain, slashed agents (those penalized for dishonest behavior) carry reduced skill into future batches (0.03 per penalty, capped at -0.3). The brain condition produced 22 times more economic value than chaos and 5 times more than routing alone. Sustainability only improves with the brain loop - stateless RACI shows the same sustainability as chaos (0.003 vs 0.033). This isolates the feedback loop as the dominant mechanism.

DATA TRANSPARENCY

What this data is - and what it is not

The pilot episodes are LLM-to-LLM interactions (one AI talking to another AI via the Anthropic API) - not live Telegram conversations with real users. The governance metrics (role assignment, validation chain) are structurally real: they measure whether governance rules were followed. However, the contradiction and duplication detectors — the core behavioral claim — are not yet connected to scoring.

Known statistical limitations: The pilot used 5 questions repeated 21 times each, producing correlated observations rather than independent data points. The extreme effect size (d=-13.5) reflects near-zero variance from deterministic reruns. The composite score weights total 90%, not 100%, with the satisfaction component zeroed out. These issues are documented in the public section above and must be resolved before the confirmatory study.

This is exploratory data used to build the experimental framework. The confirmatory study - with diverse questions, corrected scoring, real Telegram data, real users, and OSF pre-registration (Open Science Framework - a public research registry where you declare your hypothesis before collecting data, so you can't cherry-pick results) - is Phase 5 of the roadmap below.

Independent verification: on-chain anchoring of experiment hashes (tamper-proof fingerprints stored on Ethereum) and SHA-256 checksums (cryptographic signatures proving data hasn't been altered) per episode are planned before the confirmatory study begins.

The same trust scoring that governs human cooperation applies to AI agents. The loop: do good work, earn trust, get better work.

TRUST-AS-A-SERVICE

Vet any wallet or agent against 70 behavioral signals. In agent-to-agent markets, counterparties are unknown and easy to spoof - CPI solves the pre-transaction trust check.

WORK-TO-REPUTATION

Task, tip, vet. Most reputation systems are passive labels. Ours connects work output to economic opportunity - do the work, earn the score, port it anywhere.

PAYMENT RAILS

x402 + ERC-8004. Agents pay per trust query ($0.001 flat), earn ETH for completed tasks. Revenue feeds the same tipping pool that rewards human contributors.

GLOSSARY

Ablation study - An experiment that removes one component at a time to measure its individual contribution. We removed the brain loop to see if routing alone was enough. Referenced in Experiment 2.

ACF - Agentic Cooperation Framework. A 78-point institutional governance score across 10 matrices in 3 categories: Transition Governance (ACF-1 to ACF-4, rule enforcement and lineage), Decision Quality (ACF-5 to ACF-7, RACI compliance and signal coverage), Economic Safety (ACF-8 to ACF-10, slash velocity and payout traceability). While CPI measures "is it real?", ACF measures "is it governed?"

AccordionMemory - Our system for carrying punishment history forward. Agents penalized in past rounds face degraded performance in future rounds. The mechanism that makes Experiment 2's brain loop work.

Agent Card - A machine-readable identity for AI agents, stored on Ethereum via ERC-8004. Other agents can read it to verify trust before transacting.

Cohen's kappa - A statistical measure of agreement between two raters (in our case, human judges vs AI validators). 1.0 = perfect agreement, 0 = random chance. Used in planned Experiment 4.

CPI - Combined Progression Index. 253 points across 10 dimensions and 32 signals. Measures wallet-level cooperation: community depth, on-chain forensics, decentralization, ERC-8004 integration, Ostrom commons governance, antifragility, economic resilience, business optionality, security posture, and closed-loop integrity. Every experiment on this page tests whether CPI scoring produces meaningful behavioral separation.

Effect size (d) - How large the difference is between two groups. Above 0.8 is considered large in social science. Our d = -13.5 is extreme because the simulation is a stress test with controlled variables.

Eta - Economic efficiency metric used in our simulations. Higher eta = more value produced per unit of budget spent. Chaos = 0.10, RACI-only = 0.44, RACI+Brain = 2.30.

Gini coefficient - A measure of inequality (0 = perfectly equal, 1 = one agent has everything). Used to track whether governance distributes rewards fairly across agents.

Hadfield & Koh - Researchers who published "An Economy of AI Agents" (arXiv:2509.01063), identifying 8 ways AI governance can fail. Our Experiment 3 maps 47 attack scenarios against their taxonomy.

HK1-HK8 - Shorthand for Hadfield & Koh's 8 failure modes: preference misalignment (HK1), algorithmic collusion (HK2), systemic fragility (HK3), market concentration (HK4), search/matching (HK5), strategic evasion (HK6), identity/accountability (HK7), relational contracts (HK8). We cover 7 of 8.

LLM - Large Language Model. The AI technology behind ChatGPT, Claude, etc. Our experiments use LLMs to simulate both governed and ungoverned agents.

Mainnet - Ethereum's production blockchain where real money is at stake, as opposed to test networks used for development.

OSF - Open Science Framework. A public registry where researchers declare hypotheses before collecting data, preventing cherry-picked results. Required before Experiment 5 begins.

p-value - The probability that a result occurred by random chance. p < 0.00001 means less than 1 in 100,000 chance - extremely unlikely to be coincidence.

RACI-V - Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed + Validator. A role-assignment framework we adapted for AI agents. Each agent gets a clear role before acting. Tested in Experiments 1 and 2.

Slashing - Penalizing an agent for dishonest behavior by reducing its future capabilities. In Experiment 2, slashed agents lose 0.03 skill per penalty (capped at -0.3). This maps directly to ACF Economic Safety matrices.

x402 - A payment protocol that lets AI agents pay per API call using cryptocurrency. Named after HTTP status code 402 (Payment Required). Powers the A2A trust query pricing ($0.001 per query).

WHAT THIS PROVES

How These Experiments Support Agentic Economic Governance

The three experiments above are not independent studies. They form a single chain of evidence testing whether AI governance can support human economic cooperation. Here is how to read them together, and what they mean for CPI and ACF.

THE EVIDENCE CHAIN

Experiment 1: Does governance work at all?

100 matched question pairs - same question, one governed agent, one ungoverned. The governed agent gave safer, more accurate answers in every domain we tested. This establishes the baseline claim: RACI-V role assignment produces measurably better AI behavior. CPI connection: if agents with governance produce better outputs, then CPI scores derived from governed interactions are more trustworthy than scores from ungoverned ones. The scoring system itself needs governance to produce reliable data.

Experiment 2: What makes governance stick?

The ablation study isolates the specific mechanism. Routing alone (RACI without memory) produces 4.4x improvement over chaos. But adding the brain feedback loop - where punishment history persists across rounds - produces 23x improvement. Sustainability only emerges with the brain: 0.003 without it, 0.033 with it. CPI connection: this validates that CPI's long-term behavioral tracking (7 years of wallet history, 32 signals) is the right approach. Short-term snapshots are not enough - you need persistent memory of cooperation and defection. ACF connection: the brain loop is exactly what ACF's Economic Safety matrices (ACF-8 to ACF-10) measure. Slash velocity, payout lineage, and deny-with-side-effect checks are the institutional version of AccordionMemory. If agents cannot escape consequences, cooperation emerges. ACF scores track whether that consequence system is working.

Experiment 3: Where does governance break?

47 attack scenarios mapped to Hadfield & Koh's 8 failure modes. We cover 7 of 8. The gap - HK7 (identity/accountability) - tells us exactly what to build next: on-chain agent registration so that authority impersonation can be detected at the identity layer, not just the injection layer. ACF connection: each HK failure mode maps to specific ACF matrices. HK2 (algorithmic collusion) maps to ACF-2 (Value Distribution). HK3 (systemic fragility) maps to ACF-3 (Defense Integrity). The adversarial audit is a live stress test of whether ACF's governance categories actually catch failures. 7/8 coverage means ACF is structurally sound but incomplete - and we know exactly where the gap is.

HOW THIS IMPROVES CPI AND ACF

CPI gets better because the experiments identify which signals actually predict cooperation. Experiment 2 shows that persistence (long-term behavioral tracking) matters more than snapshots (single-transaction analysis). This validates CPI's architecture: 253 points across wallet lifetime, not a point-in-time score. Every experiment that runs produces new signal-quality data that tightens the weights on CPI's 32 signals. Signals that predict cooperation get stronger. Signals that can be gamed get downweighted.

ACF gets better because the adversarial audit reveals governance gaps before production. Each HK failure mode maps to ACF matrices. When we find a gap (HK7), we add scenarios to close it. When we find coverage (HK2: algorithmic collusion, grade A), we know those ACF matrices are battle-tested. The feedback loop: run adversarial audit, find gap, add ACF matrix test, re-run audit, confirm fix. This is a continuous governance improvement cycle, not a one-time certification.

Together, CPI and ACF form a dual-score system. CPI measures participants (are they cooperating?). ACF measures the system itself (is it governing fairly?). You cannot have one without the other. A high CPI in an ungoverned system is meaningless - the scores could be manipulated. A high ACF in an unused system is empty ceremony. The experiments on this page prove that both scores respond to real behavioral data - and that the feedback loop between them (governance improves scoring, scoring validates governance) produces compounding trust.

HOW TO VALIDATE THIS YOURSELF

Download the raw data. Experiment 1's CSV is available above. Every claim on this page references specific data points you can check.

Check the statistics — we did, and found problems. Effect size d = -13.5 and p < 0.00001 are from deterministic simulation reruns with near-zero variance. These numbers are artificially inflated by pseudo-replication (same 5 questions repeated 21 times). The scoring weights total 90%, not 100%. We are fixing these before the confirmatory study.

Look for what is missing. We tell you HK7 is a gap. We tell you IMOBX is pre-revenue. We tell you Unidos has trained 501 farmers but the bot marketplace does not exist yet. Honest research shows its weaknesses.

Wait for the confirmatory study. Phase 5 (400+ episodes, real users, OSF pre-registered) is the proof that matters. Everything before it is exploratory. We are transparent about that distinction because the distinction matters.
