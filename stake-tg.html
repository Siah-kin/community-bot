Frequently Asked Questions

What is BONZI staking?
You lock BONZI in HARDSTAKE. While it stays locked, your share of the pool can earn ETH from trading fees that flow through Ethervista. This page is Bonzivista’s staking workflow (connect wallet, stake, claim, withdraw).
How does Ethervista relate to this page?
Ethervista ships the routers and pool contracts Bonzi plugs into on Ethereum mainnet. Bonzivista only hosts this staking screen inside Telegram/browser. Want the official vendor story? Read Ethervista: how it works.
How are rewards paid?
ETH builds up against your stake over time. When you tap Claim, one wallet transaction sends whatever is currently owed into your wallet. Nothing auto-sends unless you confirm that claim.
Is there a lock period?
Yes. Withdrawals wait 14 days after you stake (or restake) before BONZI can leave the pool. Claiming ETH does not restart that countdown.
How is APR calculated?
When the backing numbers look solid we may show one pool-wide percent for the Yield tile. When data is noisy we instead show ETH paid over time converted to ETH per year (pacing only, whole pool math, not “your locked rate”). Connecting your wallet does not rewrite those headline tiles.
Why does Yield show small ETH/year when posts quote huge totals?
This page favors plain ETH pacing for the HARDSTAKE pool slice we index. Loud posts mix US dollars, other tokens, longer time spans, blended products, or fees outside Bonzi staking. Compare apples to apples first: denomination (ETH vs USD) plus what pool or product they counted.
Which blockchain is this?
Ethereum mainnet (chain ID 1). If your wallet is on another chain, reconnect on Ethereum.
Why claim before staking more or withdrawing?
Adding stake or pulling BONZI changes your internal reward index math. Grab pending ETH rewards first so nothing gets reset unexpectedly (same warning banner on this page).
