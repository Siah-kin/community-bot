The loop

One path, four steps. You do the first one. The system carries the rest, and each step leaves a record you can check later.

ticket → case → reviewed reply → receipt

1
Ticket. You send what broke: a bug, a broken button, a confusing flow. A screenshot or link helps. This is the only step that is yours. Record: your ticket, with a number and a time.
2
Case. Your ticket becomes a case. If other people reported the same thing, yours joins theirs, so one fix can answer many. You are not shouting into a void. Record: the case, linked to your ticket.
3
Reviewed reply. A draft answer may be prepared, but it does not reach you on its own. A person reads it first and decides if it is right. This is the step that makes it reviewed work, not a bot guessing at you. Record: who reviewed, and what they decided.
4
Receipt. A receipt ties the path together: the ticket, the review, the reply, the outcome. Something you can point to later, not a promise you take on faith. Record: the receipt itself, proof the loop ran.
 1  ticket   ●  recorded
       │
 2  case     ●  recorded
       │
 3  reply    ●  drafted
       │
       └─ a person reviews  ◀── the gate
       │
 4  receipt  ●  proof the loop ran

 ● = a record you can check

Send a useful ticket

  1. Open the bot.
  2. Type /ticket.
  3. Say what you did, what you expected, what happened.
  4. Add a screenshot or a link if it helps.
Never send seed phrases, private keys, passwords, or payment details. Keep those to yourself. A bug report never needs them.

After you send it

  • It becomes a case.
  • Similar reports may be grouped.
  • A draft reply may be prepared.
  • A person reviews it.
  • You get a reply when the answer is ready.
  • Target: within 48h.

Receipts

A receipt records the path. The ticket, the review, the reply, the outcome. It is something you can point to later, not a promise you have to take on faith.

FAQ

Is this a chatbot?

It answers, but a reply that affects you is reviewed by a person first. It is not a fire-and-forget bot.

Does it auto-send replies?

No. A person reviews the reply before it goes out. Live confirmation of that send is still being checked.

What should I put in a bug report?

What you did, what you expected, what happened. A screenshot or a link helps a lot.

How long does a reply take?

The target is within 48h.

Can I earn from reporting bugs?

Not yet. That comes later. There is no automatic reward today.

What should I never send?

Seed phrases, private keys, passwords, payment details. Keep those to yourself.

Glossary

Plain meanings for the words on this page.

Vetting
Checking that something works before you trust it. You vet by testing it yourself, not by being told it works.
Closed alpha
An early, invite-only test. Parts work, with rough edges. You are here to find them.
Ticket
A report you send when something breaks or needs a look. It gets a number and a time.
Case
What your ticket becomes once it is in the system. Similar tickets can be grouped into one case.
Reviewed reply
An answer a person reads and approves before it reaches you. Not auto-sent.
Receipt
A short record of a path: the ticket, the review, the reply, the outcome. Something you can point to later.
Passage
The way in. You open it by playing the slot on the home page. It returns a signed claim.
Signed claim
A code the bot gives you that proves you came through the Passage. Save it.
Sandbox
A safe test space, separate from the live bot. Work proven only in the sandbox is not yet live.
MIT licensed
A permissive open-source license. You can read, copy, change, and run the code freely.
