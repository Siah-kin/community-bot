On-Chain Metrics

Verifiable data for evaluating token health and holder outcomes.

How to Read This Page

Why On-Chain Data?

Price charts and market caps are easy to manipulate. This page shows data recorded on Ethereum that cannot be faked: who owns tokens, how much ETH has been distributed, and how long holders actually stay. If a project claims to reward holders, the blockchain will show whether that's true.

Murad's Memecoin Checklist

Analyst Murad Mahmudov developed criteria for separating sustainable projects from pump-and-dumps: token age (survived 6+ months), holder distribution (not controlled by few wallets), diamond hands ratio (long-term holders who earn yield without selling), and conviction metrics (average hold time, retention rate). We track all of these below.

Decentralization (Howey Context)

The Howey test asks whether profits depend on "the efforts of others" - relevant for regulatory classification. Decentralized protocols where yield comes from trading fees (not team efforts) are structurally different. We show: concentration index (HHI score - under 1500 means broad distribution), top holder share, and whether yield is permissionless protocol revenue vs discretionary distributions.

What Each Section Shows

  • Ownership Distribution: Can a few wallets crash the price? How spread out is ownership?
  • Value Distribution: Has the protocol actually paid holders? How much ETH, to how many people?
  • Alpha Analytics: Momentum signals, yield comparisons, and timing data.

All data pulled from Ethereum mainnet via Dune Analytics. Query links provided for verification.
